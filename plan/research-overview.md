1. Problem-space artifacts

A. Core domain model
Slot software revolves around reels, symbols, paylines or other win patterns, bet size, bonus triggers, jackpots, and a result engine that resolves each spin. The vocabulary that matters most for our prompts is reels, paylines, scatter, wild, progressive jackpot, RTP, volatility, bonus round, free spins, multiplier, auto-play, turbo mode, demo mode, paytable, bankroll, and wager/action. RTP is the long term percentage returned over a lot of bets. Volatility is how large the up and down swings are.

B. What modern slot apps actually contain
Slot machines include daily and hourly bonuses, lucky wheels, welcome bonuses, daily challenges, missions, leagues or tournaments, leaderboards, VIP rewards, clubs or clans, live chat or gifting, collectible cards, chests, seasonal events, and frequent drops of new machines or new themes.

C. Common slot-specific feature language
Concrete game-feature phrases you can reuse in prompts include: Random Wilds, multiplier Wilds, Guaranteed Wilds meter, 10 Free Games bonus, pick feature, bonus wheel, progressive jackpots, mini/minor/major/maxi/grand jackpots, scatter slots, and bonus-trigger symbols. Those labels appear directly in current slot content and are useful prompt ingredients because they sound natural to the category.

D. Visual-theme library
The most reusable visual themes are:
Classic Vegas: neon, 777s, cherries, bells, luxe purple/red/gold, casino-floor glow.
Fantasy/mystical: enchanted forests, magic symbols, glowing crystals, mythical creatures.
Seasonal/event-driven: spring, holiday, Halloween, special-event banners.
Travel/destination: world cities, planets, road-trip or “visit the next stop” framing.
Animal/frontier: buffalo, gold-rush, desert, western abundance cues.
Irish/luck: clovers, green/gold, luck meters, rainbow energy.
Branded or franchise-linked: named slot franchises, crossover iconography, exclusive branded tables or slots. (potentially can be branded after our team)

E. Economy and monetization patterns
The dominant economy patterns are welcome bonuses, free daily currency, escalating login rewards, optional in app purchases, VIP status, event currencies, boosters, limited time offers, and jackpot aspiration. Social apps emphasize maximum spinning and optional purchases. Real money apps emphasize deposit, bonus-spin, promo, and payout flows. Sweepstakes-style apps emphasize bonuses plus redemption messaging.

F. Social layer patterns
Common elements are chats, gifting, shared missions, leaderboards, event competition, private tables or friend crews, and public celebration of wins or rank. That means a slot app is often a hybrid of casino fantasy, collectible game, and lightweight social network.

2. User-related artifacts
A. What motivations show up in research
Published work on social casino users suggests several recurring motives: fun/excitement, social interaction, competition/challenge, stress relief/escape, and skill building. Paying players in social casino games were more likely than non-paying players to value social interaction, escaping worries, skill improvement, fun, and competition. Separate research also suggests some people use social casino play as a lower risk substitute to moderate gambling cravings, while certain social motives may be relevant to migration from social casino to gambling.

B. Why immersion matters to design
Put players in an immersive absorbed state in which players lose track of time or surroundings with celebratory sounds, visual flashes, win pacing, and session cadence. Have high contrast bright color design with satisfying animations.
